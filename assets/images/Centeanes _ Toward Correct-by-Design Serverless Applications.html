About

CENTEANES is a project funded by the Agence Nationale de la Recherche (ANR). It involves academic and industrial partners, including Télécom SudParis, École polytechnique, Université Paris Cité, Sorbonne Université and ZettaScale Technology.

Objectives

While serverless computing promises to abstract away infrastructure management concerns, it remains difficult to use and reason about due to its inherently unpredictable and non-deterministic nature. Serverless environments must cope with distributed computing challenges including concurrency, unpredictable failures, and impossibility of consensus, while also introducing unique problems such as multiple function invocations from the same event, stateless execution requiring external storage access, and platform inefficiencies.

The CENTEANES project addresses these challenges from the perspectives of correctness, efficiency, and expressivity in real application contexts. The project develops:

  • A formal framework to study the foundations of serverless computing, including function composition and fault-tolerance
  • A lightweight runtime environment where stateful operations and data locality are first-class citizens
  • A verification toolchain to program and verify serverless applications, simplifying development and enforcing correctness

The design is informed by and validated against benchmarks and full-scale industrial cloud and edge applications built with Eclipse Zenoh
